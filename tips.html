Self Storage Tips for Iron Range Residents

Practical advice for storing your belongings in Keewatin, Hibbing, Nashwauk, and across northern Minnesota — including how to handle the long winters.

1. Choose the Right Size Unit

The biggest mistake renters make is either renting too small (and not fitting everything) or too large (and paying for empty space). Here's a quick guide for Iron Range residents:

  • 8×10 ($60/mo) — boxes, seasonal gear, holiday décor. About the size of a large walk-in closet.
  • 10×10 ($85/mo) — our most popular. Fits a 1-bedroom apartment or a full two-car garage cleanout.
  • 12×10 ($90/mo) — 2-bedroom home worth of belongings, large furniture, or contractor equipment.
  • 10×24 ($115/mo) — 3+ bedroom household, vehicles, boats, snowmobiles, or business inventory.
💡 Pro Tip: When in doubt, go one size up. It's much easier to organize your unit with extra space, and the price difference is small.

2. Pack Smart — Especially for Minnesota Winters

Northern Minnesota winters are brutal. Temperatures in Keewatin and Hibbing regularly drop below -20°F. Here's how to protect your belongings in a non-climate-controlled outdoor unit:

  • Use plastic bins instead of cardboard boxes — cardboard absorbs moisture during freeze-thaw cycles.
  • Wrap furniture in moving blankets, not plastic wrap — plastic traps moisture and causes mold.
  • Place a wooden pallet on the floor to keep items off the concrete during spring thaw.
  • Don't store liquids, paint, or electronics that will be damaged by extreme cold.
  • Leave small aisles between items so you can access things without unloading the whole unit.
❄️ Iron Range Tip: Drain all water from hoses, lawn equipment, and outdoor power tools before storing. Frozen water will crack them.

3. Maximize Your Unit Space

Getting the most out of your storage unit means planning your layout before you move things in:

  • Stack boxes floor to ceiling using uniform box sizes — heavy boxes on the bottom.
  • Disassemble furniture — take apart bed frames, dining tables, and shelving units to save significant space.
  • Store vertically — lean mattresses and large mirrors against the wall rather than lying flat.
  • Put frequently accessed items near the front and seasonal/archived items in the back.
  • Label every box on the side, not the top — you'll be glad you did when things are stacked.

4. What to Store vs. What NOT to Store

Not everything belongs in a storage unit. Here's a quick breakdown:

  • ✅ Store: furniture, clothing, seasonal décor, tools, recreational equipment, business records, appliances
  • ✅ Store: ATVs, snowmobiles, boats (in our 10×24 unit)
  • ❌ Do NOT store: food, perishables, flammables, hazardous chemicals, live plants, or anything illegal
  • ❌ Do NOT store: irreplaceable items (important documents, photos, heirlooms) without proper protection

5. Use Your Unit Year-Round — Not Just for Moving

Many Keewatin and Iron Range residents use self storage for ongoing needs, not just during a move:

  • Seasonal swaps — store summer furniture and equipment in winter, and vice versa
  • Decluttering — clear out garages and basements without throwing things away
  • Business overflow — inventory, equipment, and tools for contractors and small businesses
  • Renovation storage — protect furniture while renovating rooms in your home
  • Estate cleanout — temporary storage while sorting through a family member's belongings

6. How to Protect Important Items

A few extra precautions go a long way, especially in Minnesota's climate:

  • Use a quality padlock — disc locks are harder to cut than traditional padlocks
  • Place silica gel packets near moisture-sensitive items like documents, photos, and electronics
  • Cover upholstered furniture with breathable furniture covers (not plastic)
  • Wrap fragile items in packing paper, not newspaper (ink transfers)
